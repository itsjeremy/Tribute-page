Timeline of Achievements

1968  Niel Degrasse Tyson was born in New York City

1976  Graduated from the Bronx High School of Science

1991  He earned a bachelor's degree in Physics from Harvard University and a doctorate in astro  physics from Columbia University

1995  After spending some time doing post-doctorate work at Princeton University  ,he began working at the Hayden Planetarium.

2000  Tyson completed a $210 million project to renovate the Planetarium which included design work and raising funds

2001  President George W Bush appoints Tyson as presidential advisor overseeing a commision on the future of the aerospace industry

2006  The Astronomical Union followed Tyson's lead in labeling Pluto as a dwarf planet after he ommitted it from a display of planets

2007  He published his book Death by Black Hole and Other Cosmic Quandaries and The Pluto Files: The Rise and Fall of America's Favorite Planet

2011  Mr. Tyson finnished up his 5 year run as host of PBS's NOVA ScienceNow documentary series

2014  He now served another commision to examine U.S. policy on space exploration

2014  Also in this year, Tyson was the host and executive editor of a 13-episode series called COSMOS: A Space-Time Odyssey

2015  The U.S. National Academy of Sciences awarded Tyson the Public Welfare medal for "extraordinary role in exciting the public about the wonders of science"

Until this day, Niel DeGrasse Tyson has made numerous appearances in movies and on television. Because of his charaismatic behavior and humor, he is a highly sought after guest.
